# Northern Shield — North Star

*Design principles · check every feature against this list*

These principles outrank individual documents, agent opinions, and prototype convenience. When in conflict, **the north star wins**.

---

## 1. The fortress is the main character

The player’s emotional anchor is the **home** — its walls, its halls, its scars, its skyline. Heroes matter because they **defend a place worth defending**. UI, art, and progression should make the fortress more **present** over time, not more abstract.

**Ask:** Does this feature make the fortress feel more like a living character?

---

## 2. Preparation wins battles

Skill expression lives **before** the horn blows: who stands at which gate, what was repaired, what was researched, what was forged. Combat is the **verdict** on preparation, not a second chance to redesign the fortress.

**Ask:** Does this reward planning, roster choices, and fortress investment more than twitch?

---

## 3. Heroes become legends

Defenders are not stat bundles. They earn **names, ranks, scars, titles, and saga entries**. Progression should be **felt in identity**, not only in +10% damage.

**Ask:** Will the player remember this hero after the battle?

---

## 4. Every system strengthens the fortress fantasy

Economy, combat, buildings, diplomacy — all roads lead to **“my kingdom endures.”** If a system could exist in any generic TD, it needs a Norse fortress reason to exist.

**Ask:** Why does this belong in *our* stronghold, not a generic defense game?

---

## 5. Buildings unlock systems

Structures are not passive stat sticks. A **Watch Tower** unlocks intel; a **Forge** unlocks crafting; a **Great Hall** unlocks roster depth. The player should learn the fortress by **building it**.

**Ask:** What new decision or emotion does this building introduce?

---

## 6. Progression should be visual

Tiers must be **seen** — palisade to stone to rune-etched wall; village smoke to citadel spires. Numbers without silhouette change fail this principle.

**Ask:** Can a screenshot from hour 1 and hour 50 be told apart instantly?

---

## 7. Complexity belongs between battles, not during battle

War Camp, fortress prep, crafting, research, diplomacy — **depth off the field**. Battle stays readable: who is in danger, which gate is failing, what the plan was.

**Ask:** Am I adding UI load during the wave, or during the breath between waves?

---

## 8. Every battle tells a story

Debrief is not a score screen. It is **saga**: who held, who fell, what broke, what was saved. Chronicle, scars, and fortress damage persist into the next chapter.

**Ask:** Does the player get a sentence they want to retell?

---

## 9. One screen, one question

Command map → *which fight?* War Camp → *who are we?* Fortress prep → *how do we hold?* Battle → *did it work?* After action → *what did it cost?*

**Ask:** Is this screen mixing two fantasies?

---

## 10. Resources have jobs

Gold does not solve every problem. Wood, stone, iron, food, reputation, and ancient knowledge exist so the player **prioritizes**, not hoards one number.

**Ask:** Which layer of the economy does this spend — and what does it refuse to buy?
