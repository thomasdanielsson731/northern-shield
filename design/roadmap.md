# Northern Shield — Long-Term Roadmap

*Fortress phases · systems · emotions · goals*

This roadmap describes **eras of the fortress**, not sprint tickets. Each phase changes what the player **wants**, not only what numbers go up.

Implementation order may shuffle; **emotional order should not**.

---

## Phase 1 — Village

**Fantasy:** *A handful of survivors around a fire. The North is already hungry.*

### Player goal
Survive the first assaults. Learn that **one hero at one gate** can matter.

### New systems
- Single defensive post mental model (one gate, one hero)
- Wooden palisade, tiny treasury, watch tower
- Named starter warband (attachment seed)
- Tutorial assault with forgiving intel

### New emotions
- Fragility — everything can break
- Intimacy — you know every defender by name
- First pride — “we still stand”

### What changes in play
- Preparation is **simple but total**: no wrong screen, no build grid
- Loss hurts because there is **no spare wall**
- Victory feels like **cheating death**, not optimization

### Exit condition (design)
Player clears first region feeling: *“We are not a camp anymore — we are a settlement.”*

---

## Phase 2 — Settlement

**Fantasy:** *Smoke from hearths. Neighbors send refugees. The warband is a family.*

### Player goal
Expand roster and **first stone structures**. Choose which gate culture to prioritize.

### New systems
- Multi-post fortress prep (gates, walls, watch)
- **Wood + food** campaign economy layer
- Barracks / recruit cadence
- Fortress roles (gatekeeper, scout, quartermaster)
- War Camp as **home screen** — no battlefield visible

### New emotions
- Responsibility — more names to protect
- Tradeoffs — feed people or repair walls
- Belonging — the settlement has a identity

### What changes in play
- Assault intel becomes **directional** (west front vs north front)
- Composition warnings — advisor voice, not math
- First **visual upgrade** of palisade → rough stone

### Exit condition
Player holds a **multi-front command map** and thinks: *“We need real walls.”*

---

## Phase 3 — Fortress

**Fantasy:** *Stone rings the hill. Enemies send siege, not raids.*

### Player goal
Hold against **multi-wave assault chains** while investing in permanent structures.

### New systems
- Stone / iron campaign resources
- Great Hall, Treasury, Forge as **meta buildings** (unlock systems)
- Gate integrity per facing persists between assaults
- Chronicle + scars + promotion titles
- Siege posts (ballista, catapult) as **choices**, not tile spam

### New emotions
- Stewardship — the fortress is **property**, not a map
- Grief — veteran deaths leave gaps in the wall line
- Determination — breaches are **wounds to heal**, not game over

### What changes in play
- Preparation depth matches threat depth
- Battle is **read-only plan execution** in campaign
- After action is **prose-first** — MVP, gate damage, who held

### Exit condition
Region arc complete; fortress silhouette reads **“fortress”** on the command map.

---

## Phase 4 — Stronghold

**Fantasy:** *Allies and envy. Trade caravans. The fortress is a regional power.*

### Player goal
Manage **multiple pressures** — assaults, supply, reputation — without drowning in battle UI.

### New systems
- **Reputation** — kingdom progress, faction stance
- Research tree (ancient knowledge)
- Crafting (weapons / armor tiers)
- Hero equipment depth beyond runes
- Diplomacy hooks (aid, tribute, betrayal — light at first)

### New emotions
- Political weight — not every fight is worth fighting
- Aspiration — recruits want to serve **your** banner
- Anxiety — neglecting a front has **narrative** cost

### What changes in play
- Command map gains **non-combat nodes** (events, trade)
- Economy stops being “spend gold until solved”
- Hero legend ranks become **visible** (Captain, Jarl)

### Exit condition
Player identifies with **banner and hall**, not just DPS.

---

## Phase 5 — Citadel

**Fantasy:** *Rune-light on the ramparts. Magic returns to the North.*

### Player goal
Defend against **mythic threats** with fortress spells and dragon-grade defenses.

### New systems
- Rune walls, fortress spell slots
- Hero spells tied to legend rank
- Dragon defense platforms
- Elite enemy archetypes (jötnar, draugr lords, storm hosts)
- Fortress **living city** districts (visual density)

### New emotions
- Awe — the citadel is wonder, not just stone
- Dread — enemies match your legend
- Sacrifice — high magic costs **something permanent**

### What changes in play
- Preparation includes **spell loadout** and mana/geas costs
- Battle readability relies on **prep clarity**, not more HUD
- Chronicle writes **mythic** chapters

### Exit condition
Final region unlocked; player feels ready for **last war**.

---

## Phase 6 — Last Bastion

**Fantasy:** *The last magical citadel. If the wall falls, the age ends.*

### Player goal
Complete the **100-region saga** (or endless survival variant) with a fortress that **embodies every choice** made.

### New systems
- Capstone fortress form — unique per campaign path
- Legacy succession — legends pass titles to recruits
- World map epilogue states
- New Game+ as **new jarl, same scarred fortress** (optional)

### New emotions
- Gravity — every assault is history
- Completion — the skyline tells your story
- Bittersweet victory — the North survives **because** of scars

### What changes in play
- No new mechanical complexity — **payoff** of prior systems
- Difficulty is **narrative pressure**, not cheap HP
- End screen is **saga export**, not rank

---

## Cross-phase rules

1. **Never skip emotional rungs** — Village intimacy before Citadel awe
2. **Unlock systems through buildings**, not level gates alone
3. **Each phase adds prep depth**, not battle button count
4. **Visual tier must change** at every phase boundary
