# Northern Shield — Vision

*Foundation document · permanent reference*

## One sentence

**Build and command a living Viking fortress that evolves from a tiny wooden village into the last magical citadel protecting the North.**

## What this game is

Northern Shield is not a tower defense game in the player’s mind. It is a **fortress saga**: the stronghold grows, scars, and remembers; the people who defend it become legends; every assault is a chapter in a longer story.

The **fortress is the main character**. Heroes are its voice and sword. The player is the **jarl** who chooses where to invest hope — walls, people, knowledge, or mercy.

## What the player should feel

| Moment | Emotion |
|--------|---------|
| First night survived | Relief — *we exist* |
| First named hero falls | Loss — *that was Gunnar* |
| First wall breach | Fear — *the home is wounded* |
| First fortress tier upgrade | Pride — *we are becoming something* |
| Late-game citadel assault | Weight — *if we fall, the North falls* |

## What we are not optimizing for

- Speed and reflex as primary skill
- Optimal tile placement as the fantasy
- Roguelite full resets that erase attachment
- Infinite horizontal content without vertical fortress growth
- Gold as the answer to every design question

## Relationship to the playable prototype

The current codebase proves **combat**, **roster persistence**, **campaign assaults**, and **chronicle**. Those systems are scaffolding. Future work must bend toward this vision: **preparation between battles**, **visual fortress evolution**, and **heroes as legends** — not more towers on a grid.

## Success criteria (design, not metrics)

A feature belongs in Northern Shield if it makes the player:

1. Care more about the **fortress as home**
2. Care more about **individual defenders**
3. Feel that **preparation** was meaningful
4. Read the **aftermath** as story, not spreadsheet

If it only makes combat busier, it waits.
