COLORED BASEPLATES — Northern Shield

 GOAL:
     Visually distinguish tower types on the grid even without sprites.
     Baseplates ground towers in the terrain and read instantly at a glance.

 CURRENT STATE:
     Towers sit on the grid with no ground indicator.
     Selected tower shows range circle but nothing else.
     TOWER_DEFS[type].color is already defined — use it.

 PROPOSED APPROACH:
     Draw a small rounded-rect baseplate behind each tower sprite.
     Color: derived from TOWER_DEFS[type].color with low opacity (~0.25)
     Size: CELL_SIZE x CELL_SIZE (14x14px), radius 2

 IMPLEMENTATION:
     In Tower.draw(ctx), before calling the _drawXXX method:

         const def = TOWER_DEFS[this.type];
         ctx.save();
         ctx.globalAlpha = 0.22;
         ctx.fillStyle = def.color;
         // drawRoundedRect at (this.cx - 7, this.cy - 7, 14, 14, 2)
         ctx.fill();
         ctx.restore();

     For walls: use a similar baseplate with the wall color (#6644aa)

 SELECTED TOWER VARIANT:
     When tower is selected (selectedTower===tower):
     - Increase opacity to 0.50
     - Add a 1px border stroke in the tower's def.color at full opacity

 RULES:
 - Baseplate drawn BEFORE the tower sprite/shape — never on top
 - Do not change grid.js or renderer.js for this feature
 - Keep it subtle: the sprite is the focus, the baseplate is ambient
