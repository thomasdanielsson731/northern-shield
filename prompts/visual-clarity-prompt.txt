Prioritize gameplay readability in Northern Shield. Visual style: Norse dark fantasy.

 ESTABLISHED COLOR LANGUAGE:
     Graveborn: body #6628a8 (deep purple), glow rgba(160,60,255,0.55)
     Wisp:       body #88bbff (ethereal blue), glow rgba(140,190,255,0.95) — signals flying
     Golem:     body #5c4030 (stone brown), amber core #c07820, amber eyes
     Banshee: body #00eeff (cyan), EMP pulse ring animation on discharge
     Shield Wall: #404060 (dark stone), slow aura ring
     Berserker: sprite-rendered (barbarian_sprites.png), fireFlash triggers ATTACK frame
     Valkyrie:   sprite-rendered (valkyria_sprites.png)
     Archer:       sprite-rendered (archer_sprites.png)
     Catapult:   sprite-rendered (dvarg_sprites.png)
     Blondie:     sprite-rendered (brynhild_sprites.png)
     Wall cell:   #1e1430 (dark stone purple)
     Spawn cell: #6a3e08 (amber portal gate)
     Goal cell:   #580810 (deep crimson altar)
     Path:         rgba(200,150,35) warm gold glow
     UI panels:   rgba(6,3,16) dark fill + rgba(210,160,40) gold border + corner diamonds
     Background: #06030f → #0e0820 gradient + pulsing purple and amber glows

 SHAPE LANGUAGE (must stay distinguishable at 14px cell size):
     Graveborn: circle — common, fast, forgettable
     Wisp:       rotated square (diamond) — distinctive, signals bypass threat
     Golem:     large circle (radius 11) with rings + glowing core — unmistakably boss-tier
     Banshee: circle with EMP pulse ring — signals threat to towers

 SPRITE SYSTEM:
 - 4-frame strips: frame 0=IDLE, 1=WALK, 2=ATTACK, 3=DEATH
 - drawSpriteFrame(ctx, key, frame, x, y, aimAngle, dw) — flips horizontally when aiming left
 - fireFlash > 0 → show frame 2 (ATTACK), otherwise frame 0 (IDLE)
 - SPRITES registry checked before use: if (!SPRITES[key]) fall back to canvas draw

 CLARITY RULES:
 - Tower range rings: dashed, tower color at low opacity — visible but not distracting
 - HP bars: green (>50%) → yellow (>25%) → red — universal language
 - Wisp ethereal glow signals "this bypasses your walls"
 - Golem amber eyes + molten core signal "this is a boss"
 - Banshee cyan signals "this zaps your towers"
 - Tower sprite flips to face target — always shows what each tower is shooting
 - Path highlight (golden) shows where the enemy route goes

 The player should instantly understand at a glance:
 - Enemy movement direction and relative speed
 - Which enemies are threats and why
 - Tower attack range
 - Projectile trajectories
 - Damage state of each enemy

 Clarity over visual complexity. Never add an effect that obscures enemy readability.
